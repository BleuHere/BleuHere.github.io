LEARNING PLAN

by Bleuette

What is your long term goal or career pathway?

My long term goal is to work in the social impact space, utilising technology to make support more accessible to minority groups, especially women and the LGBT+ community, and to combat the climate crisis. I see myself doing this by working for apps and organisations who already work in this space, and one day building an organisation with a committed team and create a piece of technology that fills a need in these communities.

A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning

Strengths:

  • well organised and works well in a routine
  • ability to connect dots and link new knowledge to existing concepts
  • enjoyment found in puzzles and challenges

Weakness:

  • bit of a bash-bash find-the-solution approach as opposed to a curious approach when things go wrong
  • high expectations means I often fall short of my own mark and feel a bit shit about it
  • stubborn and finds it tricky to let things go if I haven’t achieved what I set out to do

What skills (non-technical core/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

I would love to develop my team-work skills. I know that in cases where I like my team-mates I can really thrive, but if I don’t easily connect with my team-mates I can find this distracting. I would love to develop good tools and strategies for genuinely connecting with people I don’t naturally gravitate towards.

I’d also love to develop leadership skills. I would love to work in a leadership position and learning more about how value-driven leadership works in a real-world sense would be really impactful in my learning journey.

A commitment to how you will manage your workload:

An explanation of how you will manage yourself to work productively and safely with other learners, Facilitators and industry/community representatives

I will work productively and safely with others by keeping in touch with my mind and body and applying coping mechanisms to self-regulate if I feel activated, distracted or ‘off’. This may look like logging off discord for a little while, taking a walk or connecting with somebody and having a chat.

I will focus on kindness, gentleness and compassion towards myself and others.

I will be mindful of my space-taking during classtime and ask questions where relevent. I will respect the time and energy of my classmates and facilitators by ensuring my contribution to discussions is pertinent and useful.

What you will do when things are building up and starting to get to you?

I will remind myself that I have been preparing for this and that I can get through this! I will take breaks and move my body. I will prioritise rest and physical wellbeing.

In moments of tension I will take myself to a safe spot (physically or in my mind) and allow myself to regluate before I get back into what I was doing. This might look like going for a walk, venting to a willing friend, having a cry or just gently reminding myself that discomfort is okay.

I will communicate my needs with class-mates/team-mates and/or facilitators when necessary, and be self-reliant where possible. This looks like - self care as above, or using google, MDN, StackOverflow and other tools to help me solve technical issues.

When self-reliance actually looks like asking for help, I will turn to my academic and wellbeing support network - facilitators, friends, family and partner.

A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way:

Where:

When I am stuck and have exhausted my allocated time and/or energy resources.

How:

I’ll reach out to my classmates in discord first, then, I’ll chat to my partner who is a senior software engineer. If I am still unclear or haven’t met the learning objective then I will contact a facilitator (either in the help requests discord channel or contact my cohort facilitator directly.

I know that I will have moments of learning struggle. The big hurdle for me will be my reaction to my learning and I anticipate some mental struggles. Fortunately, I have a great counsellor who I can talk to, and I have great home, partner and familial support to be there for me if it starts feeling like too much.

A description of what you expect from the Facilitation team:

I expect responsiveness within school hours. I expect the team to be emotionally aware and gentle. I expect great learning analogies, and clear expecations of deliverables. I expect to be helpfully nudged in the right direction if I am really stuck. I expect compassion and patience and humility. I expect that celebration is a part of the facilitation team’s values, but that validation isn’t given willy-nilly. I really want to feel safe to make mistakes, and to not be given the answer if I ask for help, but offered an approach, a direction or a gentle prompt. I want to know that I am safe to feel my feelings and that I am supported or can access support if I ask.

Any scheduling information such as block-out times when you are committed to other things. For example, you might have whānau commitments like school drop-offs. It's important to plan these in.

I cook dinner for my flatmates. During bootcamp this will be once per week. I also need to schedule time to walk my dog, but this is flexible. I have one or two appointments that I can’t reschedule.


LEARNING PLAN 2.0

What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

I’m hoping to become a developer! I would love to work on solving pertinent issues that humans and the planet face. I think I’m much more interested in the back end structuring and functionality, but the truth is that I don’t really know yet. It’s exciting to know I will get to try lots of stuff!

I would love to be a part of making useful SaaS technology that could combat things like climate change and inequalities in the human world. I’m really interested in the impact fashion has on people and the planet, and would love to work on creating solutions around this. Other causes I feel strongly about are gender equality and climate change. I’ve been looking into carbon credits which seems pretty cool, but I’m curious about what else is out there.

What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

I think that my strengths and limitations have changed! I know that throughout Foundations I’ve been really focussing on how I respond to my being stuck and feeling stupid. I’ve noticed a big difference in my reaction to those thoughts and feelings, and feel like I have been able to disarm those thoughts through self awareness and kindness to myself. I’m still working on letting lingering pestering feelings and thoughts go… Bootcamp will be great practice for this.

A strength I’ve noticed in myself is healthy resource management. I’ve been able to recognise when I need a shorter day due to mental or physical needs, and I can manage my workload well while I also tend to myself. I think this will be different in Bootcamp as the days will be longer and more prescribed, but I do think that honing this skill in a higher stress environment will be really beneficial to my growth.

I think my communications skills will serve me well, and I look forward to developing these further by looping and practising healthy teamwork.

I’m also really excited to do lots of learning! I was pretty nervous to start Foundations, but my brain is doing me so proud! I’ve been loving the learning and I feel like my brain is ready for more!

What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

A limitation I anticipate in Bootcamp is resilience to mental exhaustion. I struggle with mental health, and find that if my brain is tired it’s really hard for me to focus. During Foundations I’ve been able to manage my time around these days, but I do feel nervous for those days in Bootcamp when there’s lots to do and learn but my mental capacity is limited.

What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

I’m really excited to work in intentional teams. I’ve worked in teams before, but haven’t really felt prepared to work well with different personalities and have struggled to develop skills on the fly in those situations. I’m really looking forward to working with all of my classmates and learning about how they want to interact and how they feel safe in a team.

I’m nervous for peer programming because it challenges me to be vulnerable in front of others, so I’m looking forward to feeling more comfortable in this setting.

I think my Foundations expectations will continue on into Bootcamp. As below:

I expect responsiveness within school hours. I expect the team to be emotionally aware and gentle. I expect great learning analogies, and clear expectations of deliverables. I expect to be helpfully nudged in the right direction if I am really stuck.

I expect compassion and patience and humility. I expect that celebration is a part of the facilitation team’s values, but that validation isn’t given willy-nilly. I really want to feel safe to make mistakes, and to not be given the answer if I ask for help, but offered an approach, a direction or a gentle prompt. I want to know that I am safe to feel my feelings and that I am supported or can access support if I ask.

What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp

I expect to lean into the learning and give heaps of energy to this. I expect myself to stay open and curious. I expect that I will have bad days, and know that I am great at supporting myself and asking for help.

I expect myself to maintain a good routine. I expect to allow myself to rest when I need to. I expect that I will face discomfort with others and will seek healthy ways to deal with this.

I expect to finish the course and I really hope to thrive during it!
